# Gratitude

I think gratitude is one of the fundamental virtues. Living with gratitude makes our existence lighter, more bearable, and infuses every single one of our experiences in the world with genuine meaning.

Because when you give thanks for everything that happens, everything has a purpose, everything carries meaning, everything has a reason for being.

Thank you, [[GOD]], for the gift of life, for the blessing of [[MIND]] and [[SPIRIT]]. I am a vessel through which Your greatness manifests, and my joy is to follow You—wherever You lead me.

I have noticed something profoundly powerful since I began to practice gratitude.

When I wake up, I give thanks simply for having energy and being able to breathe. When I meditate, I give thanks for the Peace and the wisdom that this practice—and God—bestow upon me. I began to thank Mother and Father. Mother being [[Sophia Instructions]], Father being God the Creator. I give thanks for the food that enters my body. I notice that the anxiety around eating diminishes once I thank the hands that planted the rice, the people who raised the animals, and the animals who gave their lives so I could be nourished. I give thanks for my yoga sessions and my training. I give thanks for the internet, for AI, and for the blessing of being able to dedicate my days to study instead of to labor.

When you practice gratitude, everything we once labeled as “bad” simply dissolves. What remains is only the quiet joy of being alive.
