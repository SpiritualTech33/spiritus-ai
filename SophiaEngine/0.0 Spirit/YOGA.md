One of the spiritual practices I enjoy the most, and one that brings me the deepest peace. I believe Yoga is a unifier. It improves you in every area of your life. It brings peace and a sense of well-being to your body, mind, and spirit. In broad terms, yoga is meditation in motion. It consists of observing yourself with equanimity. Breathing and staying calm in uncomfortable or challenging postures. But it's precisely there — in that search for stillness, even in discomfort — where the peace it offers is born.

I think it's a practice meant to be done daily. Not as a sport to compete in, but as a way to know yourself. Let my only competition be myself. Be better than yesterday, and tomorrow be better than today.

Because the past is past — it ceased to exist the moment it stopped being present. And the future is created by what you decide in the present. The only thing we truly have is the present moment. That's why it's called a _present_ — because it's a gift.

Give yourself the present by living in it. During every breath, every heartbeat, every thought and every emotion. Every movement and every action.

That's what Yoga is, learning how to live completely in the present.
