# GOD

## **What is God?** **Why do I feel It so present?** **Where is God?**

God—perhaps the single most recurrent theme in all of human history, and for good reason. It is the All, that which existed before any form, any thought, any word. It is the Creator, the manifestor of existence itself. And yet it is curious: we use human language to name what cannot be named. There is a line in the Tao that says something like, “The Tao that can be spoken is not the eternal Tao.” (Interpreting the Tao as God, as that divine essence.) And I believe it is so—the words are never enough to capture the infinite emanation of the Lord. Still, we make our best attempt, and that is exactly what I seek to do now: to encapsulate God in human language.

For me, God is far more than a mere presence or a being. It carries no labels, no names, no features. It simply _is_. God is everything that exists: the trees that gift this planet oxygen through the quiet miracle of photosynthesis; the rivers that pulse with life, carrying the sacred hydrology of Earth in their veins; the animals that move beneath our shared care. God is the atmosphere—our delicate shield of nitrogen, oxygen, and ozone—that protects us from the sun’s fierce ultraviolet rays and allows every breath we take. God is the Sun itself, a nuclear furnace fusing hydrogen into helium, pouring photons and raw energy across 150 million kilometers so that life may dance. God is the comets tracing their icy ellipses and the black holes bending spacetime into infinity. God is the birth and death of stars: the gravitational collapse that ignites nebulae, the supernova explosions seeding the universe with carbon, oxygen, and iron—the very elements that now form your blood and my words. God is galaxies spiraling in gravitational embrace, planets forming from cosmic dust, quasars blazing at the edge of observable reality—everything your mind can imagine and far beyond.

At the same time, God is the pure, unconditional love of a mother for her child—the neurochemical symphony of oxytocin and deep evolutionary bonding. God is the peace that settles in the hearts of the just and the kind, that quiet coherence where the nervous system rests in resonance with the cosmos. God is the creative spark that ignites inventors and dreamers, the same electrochemical lightning in the human brain that once kindled fire and now writes symphonies and code.

God is in every place and every moment, because God _is_ everything that exists.


God is sovereign, creator of all, manifester of manifestation. The reason and cause in itself of existence. Creator and created. Father and son, teacher and disciple. Unique in existence, since his existence depends on himself.
