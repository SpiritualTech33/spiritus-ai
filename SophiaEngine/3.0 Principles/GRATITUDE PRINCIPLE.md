# Gratitude Principle
---

Give more attention to the good things that happen to you than to the bad ones.

Our vision of the world ultimately becomes whatever we choose to focus our attention on.

The mind is like fertile soil: what you water grows.

What you ignore withers.

When you deliberately direct your attention toward gratitude—toward the breath that still fills your lungs, the sunrise you witnessed, the meal that nourished you, the friend who listened, the small victory in training, the quiet moment of peace—you fertilize the soil of your perception with light.

Over time, the landscape of your inner world changes:

- Problems shrink because they receive less energy.
- Blessings multiply because they receive more.
- The default lens shifts from scarcity and complaint to abundance and wonder.

Gratitude is not denial of pain.

It is a conscious choice of emphasis.

It is training the mind to notice what is already good, rather than obsessing over what is lacking or broken.

As attention goes, so goes reality.

Where you place your focus is where your experience is born.
