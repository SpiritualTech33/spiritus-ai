# Apprentice Principle
---

Always identify yourself as an apprentice.
If you are an apprentice, you will always learn something—no matter what.
It doesn’t matter if you’ve been doing something for 20 years.
You can be an apprentice with 20 years of experience.
You can be an apprentice with 40 years.
You can be an apprentice until your last breath.

Remember the Socratic premise:
“I know that I know nothing.”

That single sentence is one of the most powerful safeguards against intellectual death.
It keeps the mind porous, humble, hungry.
It prevents the hardening of certainty that turns knowledge into dogma.
Expertise without the apprentice mindset decays into arrogance.
The moment you believe you have “arrived,” you stop moving.
The moment you stop moving, you begin to rot—slowly, invisibly, from the inside.

Your skills plateau, your curiosity dies, your perception narrows, and eventually what once made you exceptional becomes mediocre because the world kept evolving while you stood still.

The true master never graduates from being a student.
He simply becomes a more advanced, more disciplined, more reverent apprentice.
