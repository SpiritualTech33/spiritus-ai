# Ontology Of Sound
---
## Association of Identity with Your Name

It’s curious, the profound association that exists between the idea we have of the “self” and the name that was given to us at birth. I mean, think about it this way. When someone calls you by your name, there’s an existential and causal relationship between the call and the sense of belonging you feel with that name. When they call us, we accept that we are what they’re calling. It’s as if our name encapsulated our identity, our idea of who we are.

There’s even something mystical about the name. It’s not something we chose, and we carry it for the rest of our lives. We create a structure of meaning, symbol, belonging, and identity with the name, as if that defined us or showed what we are. When you ask someone, “Who are you?” Almost always, the first response is their name. But if you didn’t have a name, who would you be? What kind of person would you be? How would you speak? What would you think about?

You’ll see, when you remove the name from the equation, it feels like staring into the abyss. Into that nothingness, which is everything at the same time.

With that said, I’d also like to make an observation about the power of sound. How vibrations emitted into space create meaning and identity. We already mentioned the name, which, in the end, when pronounced, is nothing more than a sound, among many that the ear can perceive.

Sound itself carries meaning. When you pronounce a word, when you say a name, or when you sing a song, you’re producing sound, vibrations in space. But that sound you emit carries a meaning. Now, the Socratic question here is, where does that meaning come from? Why do we associate a specific sound with a specific meaning?

Honestly, I don’t know, but it gives me something to think about. It leaves me thinking, if this is the case, why do we associate our identity with our name? If the meaning of the name lives outside of us.

## Association of Internal States with Internal Dialogue

On this topic, I’d love to dive deeper, because it’s something I want to remember later and keep present. The power of our internal dialogue in building and manifesting an elevated or positive internal state.

Well, the pillar of our thinking comes from internal dialogue. While it’s true that you can think without internally dialoguing, I believe that the deep abstraction of ideas comes precisely from internal dialogue, because it’s with words that we can truly abstract concepts and themes. Words have that particularity, above mental images or bodily sensations. With that said, it’s necessary to clarify that internal dialogue is essential in the development of consciousness—not just having it, but being able to observe it, to observe the patterns of internal dialogue you maintain. What kind of things do you say in your mind? What kind of words do you constantly relate to? What kind of affirmations do you execute?

You’ll see, words have immense power over our psychology, much more than you think. Think about it this way. If every day you tell yourself “I’m a fool,” “I’m not capable,” “I can’t do it,” brother, it’s obvious you won’t be able to achieve anything you set out to do, because you’re conditioning your way of facing the world through your words. If instead you tell yourself “I am capable,” “I am enough,” “I can do it,” you’ll end up doing whatever it is, because you believe you can do it.

Words shape our way of interpreting the world. Make sure to always have a positive and elevated internal dialogue. Get used to making positive affirmations that raise your vibration.

I’m not religious, but it’s mentioned that, before everything, there was the word. God said, let there be light, and light was made. Observe the verb: Said. It was words that created the light and the universe.
