## What Thinking Is?

It's curious, because in doing this, writing this note, something very concrete is happening - I'm thinking about thinking. Meta-thinking, I feel, would be the correct name. 

That said, I want to continue asking questions, because by asking, we allow the answers to come to us. What does it really mean to think? I asked some close people, siblings, and friends. Some answered that thinking is analyzing a situation to come to a resolution. A very valid answer if you think from a resolutionist perspective (the search for feasible resolutions to a concrete problem). Others couldn't tell me with certainty what thinking was. My housekeeper answered that deciding what we will eat tomorrow is a form of thinking. And it's partly true, it constitutes a decision, and decision-making itself is already established as logic and thought. My closest brother answered that thinking is philosophizing or possibility, thinking about the possibilities of something, and I consider it the closest option to what I myself think. 

Now that I find myself in this doubt, I think that I had never really questioned what it means to think. It's strange, it's something we do automatically, even unconsciously, but something strange happens when you start to think about the very act of thinking. 

If I had to give a definition with what I know up to this point in my life, I would say that thinking is the human faculty by which we are able to create meaning and form to the abstraction of existence. In general terms, thinking constitutes the reason why language, communication, philosophy, science, theoretical physics, neuroscience, spirituality, love, faith and practically any concept you can imagine exists. Everything that happens to us exists in thought. There is a Hermetic principle that I use constantly, it is the MENTALISM PRINCIPLE. There is a book that personally marked much of the way I think and interpret the world. It is called "The Kybalion" written by some self-named, 3 initiates. The book explores THE 7 HERMETIC PRINCIPLES. The first of these principles states the following: The All is Mind; The Universe is Mental. I think now, after questioning what thought is, it acquires a deeper meaning. Indeed, the All is mental, because everything that happens in our phenomenological experience, happens in the mind. When you hear something, what really happens is that your ears capture the vibrations of the environment, then those vibratory signals captured by your auditory apparatus are sent to the brain and your brain registers those signals, giving them meaning. That is thought. When you eat something, your taste buds register the chemical composition of the food, taking those signals to the brain and giving you the experience of taste. Everything that happens in your life experience is happening in your mind, it is happening in thought itself. I am pleased to have started using Obsidian with this approach, because it will allow the center of my graph to be thought itself. And once you observe this fact, it becomes an elephant in the room impossible to ignore
