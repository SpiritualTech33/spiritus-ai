# Metaphysics
---

## What is Metaphysics?

The name might sound a little intimidating—many people already fear PHYSICS because it's complex, so hearing "metaphysics" makes you think it's something even more complicated, and indeed it is. But not in a bad way, not as something incomprehensible or impossible to learn. Rather, it's because it forces anyone who studies it to do something very similar to what EPISTEMOLOGY demands: it makes you stop taking things for granted and invites you to question deeply everything you interpret as true.

Physics, for example, explains how gravity works, gives you measurements, provides examples. Metaphysics, on the other hand, comes at you with total brutality and asks: Why is there gravity? Why is there order instead of chaos? What does it mean to exist? Metaphysics is concerned with studying what lies beyond the physical.

**METAPHYSICS**

text

```
μετὰ (meta) = beyond, after
φυσική (physike) = physics, nature
**"Beyond physics"**.
```

That's why it's so intimidating: it pulls the ground of assumptions out from under the way you base your existence and leaves you in the void, asking profound questions about the first causes of things. And that's exactly why it's at the same time so fascinating and exciting. All of SCIENCE, all of PHILOSOPHY, every idea and everything we know, begins first in metaphysics—because none of it can exist without the other. Metaphysics is the questioning of existence itself, and once you start questioning what it means to exist, nothing is ever the same again.

Metaphysics doesn't study things; it studies the very fact that something _is_.

It asks what exists, what it means to exist, and what the ultimate principles of reality are. It doesn't seek final answers—it seeks well-formulated questions. And once you start asking them, you can no longer inhabit the world in the same way.
