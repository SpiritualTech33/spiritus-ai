# What Philosophy Is?

Philosophy constitutes one of the fields that fascinates me the most. I live in love with philosophy, and it's curious because philosophy, in an etymological sense, means love of knowledge. And that's exactly what I experience; I love knowing, I love learning. Philosophy, as a science, is difficult to describe since it has endless different branches into which it is divided. In my opinion, and to simplify things, I remain content with the mere fact of knowing. However, on the other hand, there are certain branches that I find fascinating. Among them, EPISTEMOLOGY, ONTOLOGY, and METAPHYSICS are the ones that arouse my greatest interest. But, respecting my exploratory spirit and my insatiable curiosity, the question remains: What is philosophy? If we go in the pure etymological sense, we obtain this answer:

- φιλο- (Philo) = love
- σοφία (Sophia) = wisdom

That is philosophy, understanding that knowledge is not possessed; it is only loved. When I develop ideas, concepts, or write about anything, that knowledge, those ideas, that intelligence, is not mine; it is borrowed. Buddhism has taught me to live from detachment. Detachment from desire, from possessions, from identity, from knowledge, and ultimately, from ego. Knowledge does not exist for us to seize it or proclaim it as our own. It is there to love it and to be amazed by it. The best way to philosophize is by accepting that we know nothing.

φιλοσοφία - Love of wisdom

It is not possessing knowledge. It is LOVING knowledge. It is not having answers. It is LIVING in the question.

```
"I only know that I know nothing"
- Socrates
```
