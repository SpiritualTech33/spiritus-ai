# JESUS

To speak of Jesus is to speak of many things at once — for he is the kind of man who cannot be catalogued or confined to a single category. His legacy is immense, and what he left to the men who came after him is a gift worthy of being called divine.

---

## Who is Jesus?

Let us begin with the basics — his name. He is universally known as Jesus, though historians agree that he was called Yeshua Bar Yosef. Jesus is the Greek transliteration of an Aramaic name. He was born into a brutal political context. Judea under Roman occupation was a pressure cooker — crushing taxes, public humiliations, routine executions. The weary people of Judea had long been waiting for a Messiah, someone to liberate them from Roman tyranny.

---

## His Philosophy — Love, Compassion and The Kingdom of God

It is remarkable that given the political fire into which Jesus was born, one would expect a messiah forged for war and revolution against the Roman empire. And yet Jesus grew into a man of light, of miracles, of hope and faith. His philosophy reflects this clearly.

### The Kingdom of God is within you, not outside you

This is perhaps the most important and valuable teaching Jesus ever gave. He said something that was difficult to interpret, yet profoundly true and revelatory — that the Kingdom of God should not be sought outside oneself, but within. Every one of us carries the divine flame in our interior — the potential, the capacity to become a vessel through which the divine manifests. But we must stop searching outward and begin the inward journey. We are all light. We are all children of God. Give yourself the gift of looking inward. Seek within your being the love, the compassion, the gratitude and the peace — and each time you do, you draw closer to God and his kingdom.

### Service as the highest expression of man

Jesus also invited us to see service not as obligation, but as divine privilege. To place your gifts in the service of humanity is the purest form of love and humanity. We were blessed at birth with gifts, and it is our responsibility to offer those gifts to our brothers and sisters.

### To love man, even our enemies

Surely you have heard something like *"Love your neighbor as yourself"* — and I believe this is one of the most powerful truths ever spoken. When we open our hearts to infinite love, when we set aside resentment, jealousy, envy and hatred, and allow our hearts to beat at the rhythm of love, everything changes. Love is the most powerful force that exists. To love above all things is an act of faith that elevates your essence closest to divinity, virtue and beatitude. Love — love every day, love with intensity, let the beauty of love flood your life and your being.

---

## The Spirit of Jesus

How do you explain that a Galilean carpenter — without an army, without wealth, without political apparatus, dead at 33 as a criminal of the state — reorganized Western civilization for 2000 years?

The spirit of Jesus lives in the minds and consciousnesses of countless people today. His legacy is undeniable, his influence more than remarkable. But Jesus can be reduced to a single truth — unconditional love. Without barriers, without borders, without conditions. Only love.

There is a scene of extraordinary power that I will never forget. I was not there, but I know in the deepest chamber of my heart that it happened as told — because I have tears in my eyes now.

As Jesus hung crucified, enduring a punishment unbearable for any of us mortals, he did not allow himself to fall into hatred or darkness. Despite the public humiliation, despite the severity of what was done to him, despite everything he bore for us — he chose to bless and forgive those who crucified him.

What further proof of love is needed to understand why Jesus is the model of man we should aspire to be on this earth? He is my model and my inspiration. I love Jesus and allow his spirit to be my guide, my anchor. I dissolve whatever darkness may surround me, because I am anchored in the spirit of Jesus.

Amen.

---

## Jesus In Me — Conclusion

I believe Jesus is the incarnation of divinity itself. He is the aspirational model toward which every man should reach. I allow Jesus to live within my being and illuminate all my days with his love.
