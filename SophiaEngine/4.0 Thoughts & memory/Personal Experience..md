# Personal Experience
---

Something really funny happened to me today. It was during the afternoon of January 17th, 2026.

Since the start of the year, I made the decision to burn my ships, kill my elephants, and tie myself to the mast.

These are metaphors I read in a book, related to overcoming your addictions or dependencies. In my case, at the beginning of 2026 I decided I wanted to quit smoking — both cigarettes and weed. This with my long-term health in mind.

Honestly, I've been handling it pretty well. Thanks to meditation, I manage to stay at peace most of the time. I have a pretty particular neurological condition. I have dyslexia — a difference in how the brain processes information. I also have ADHD, which, despite the common belief (based on outdated definitions) that it's a deficit in attention capacity, recent research has shown it's more of a hyperactivity in a person's brain activity than an actual attention deficit. That, concatenated with a deep spiritual sensitivity — due to the fact that I was literally born dead. An involuntary movement inside my mother's womb caused the umbilical cord to wrap around my neck, strangling me every time my mother tried to push. According to my mother, I was born without vital signs, and it was the doctors who resuscitated me and brought me back to life.

That said, I can declare that I have a pretty peculiar worldview. I believe our consciousness lives connected to the universe, and that the universe in turn is a giant living being — one that is aware of itself and of our own consciousness. Imagine it as a being that uses our consciousness to observe itself.

Now, using the concept of synchronicity — which I know thanks to Carl Jung — I find it very easy to spot them. They happen almost daily; you just don't notice.

Having mentioned the fact that I'm fighting the weed battle — the fight against addiction is never linear. It's a constant up and down. Well, today I went with some friends to smoke a joint at the softball field next to my house. It's a field I go to often to do yoga, run, and train. There's almost never anyone there. And I'm not joking when I say it's next to my house — it's about 30 meters from being my backyard, haha.

So I went there with some friends to smoke the damn joint. While we were at it, I was telling them about the Six Phase Meditation by Vishen Lakhiani. In a way, I guided them through the compassion phase. While they were doing it, two state police officers showed up. They approached, took photos of the weed, of the plates on my friends' motorcycle. They checked our wallets and inspected the area, looking for more marijuana. Fortunately, we had only bought just enough to roll two joints.

From the moment I heard the officers speak and saw the way they handled the situation, I could tell they weren't very professional, and that they didn't really want to cause harm either. They were just doing their job. So I adopted a very calm attitude, cooperated, and answered their questions honestly. Specifically, they asked me where I was from. Generally, because of my physical appearance, people assume I'm not Mexican. I have somewhat marked Caribbean features.

They asked what I do for a living, and I said I'm a student — studying programming. After I answered, the questioning officer's attitude toward me became noticeably calmer. As if he could sense there was no ill intent in me. In any case, I handled the whole situation with a lot of composure. I had gotten nervous at first, but thanks to meditation, I was able to observe the nerves and redirect that energy toward thinking of a peaceful resolution.

In the end, we just gave them some money — about 350 Mexican pesos, roughly 30 USD — and they left without even taking the rest of the weed. Which allowed us to roll the second joint, which I smoked on my walk back home.

As I said before, I believe in synchronicities. And I believe this was a very clear one telling me I should keep walking the path of sobriety.

Honestly, it was a fun experience — and one that showed me that power isn't in positions. It's in intelligence.
