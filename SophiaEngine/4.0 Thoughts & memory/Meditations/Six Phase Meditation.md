# Six Phase Meditation
---

I'm writing this note to remember this practice and keep myself in an optimal state, always.

I read about it in a really good book called _The Code of the Extraordinary Mind_ by Vishen Lakhiani — a type of meditation called the Six Phase Meditation. It's presented as a practice designed to meet the needs of people living in this 21st century — digitized, computerized, full of speed and stress. It pushes back, with some force, against the monastic meditation models of past centuries.

Without further delay, I'll describe the practice here, so I can keep it present and never forget it. As the name suggests, it's made up of six phases, one after the other.

## **1. Compassion**

It begins with compassion. Vishen states that compassion improves both the health and the mood of those who practice it. You start by calling up an image that evokes that feeling. It can be anything — a scene from a movie, your partner's smile, something you read in a book — anything that awakens compassion within you. For me, it's thinking of my mother. Once you've called up that image and you feel the compassion, transform it into a white light. Then bring that light to the center of your body, and visualize it radiating outward — filling the room, then your house, your neighborhood, your town, your city, your country — until it reaches the entire world, carrying the light of compassion to every corner of the planet. If you're like me, you can go further and expand that light to every corner of the cosmos. Once you hold a steady image of yourself radiating that white light across the universe, you've completed the first phase.

## **2. Gratitude**

This is a practice I've been doing for several months already, but I'll never get tired of emphasizing how powerful gratitude is for your psyche and your daily life. The phase is fairly simple: find three things to be grateful for in your personal life, three things in your professional life, and three things about yourself. It can be literally anything — being thankful for your health, for being able to breathe, for having a bed to sleep in, for the cup of coffee waiting for you before work. You can be grateful for your intelligence, your charisma, or your good sense of humor. Anything at all. The point is to train your mind to default to gratitude instead of complaint.

## **3. Forgiveness**

Vishen mentions a program in his book called 40 Years of Zen. It was a program for experienced meditators, where advanced neural sensors were placed on their heads to measure the waves their brains emitted. They found that when forgiveness was invoked, the peaks of alpha waves were significantly higher. He also states that practicing forgiveness consistently improves overall health — not just mental, but physical too.

This phase might be the hardest of all, but I can say firsthand that it's the most revitalizing and the one that generates the most peace.

It's very simple. Call up a memory of the person you want to forgive. Remember all the negative feelings that person may have caused you. Feel them again. Let them flood your awareness. Don't stay with those emotions for more than two minutes. But once you've invoked that memory and those negative feelings, release them — and picture yourself standing in that scene, embracing the person who hurt you, forgiving them from a place of love. You can say something like: _I forgive you from love, compassion, and gratitude. I release any negative emotion, because within my being there is no room for negativity._ You don't have to forgive everyone at once. Go little by little. Naturally, some people will be harder to forgive than others. But with time, you'll have released every negative emotion you were holding.

## **4. Visions of the Future**

The fourth phase is, in my personal opinion, the most fun and the most exciting. It's simple: visualize your future. That's it. Think of a version of yourself living three years from now. Where would you like to live? What experiences would you like to have? What car do you drive? What work do you do? Who do you spend your time with? What places do you visit? What does your house look like? Your bed? What food do you eat? What kind of books do you read? The more detail you bring in, the better. See yourself in the future you most desire, and give thanks as if it has already happened. No modesty here — think big.

## **5. The Perfect Day**

Alright, you've visualized your future. Now the question becomes: _what can I do today to move closer to that future?_ That's where the fifth phase comes in. Plan your day, thinking about what concrete actions you can take today to manifest the wonderful future you saw in the previous phase. But it's not just about action — also visualize the day filled with positive things. Love, peace, joy, good things all around. Program your day so that good things come to you. Become someone unshakable — no matter what happens during the day, you'll receive it with the best attitude. Hold this thought: _I can't control what happens to me, but I can control how I respond to it._ I choose to respond with love, compassion, gratitude, and positivity.

## **6. The Blessing**

The final phase, and the most important one in my opinion. The blessing. Now, imagine a golden or white light — bright and purifying — entering through the top of your head. As you receive it, carry that energy throughout your entire body. Your forehead, eyes, ears, nose, mouth, neck, shoulders, chest, arms, abdomen, back, waist, legs, thighs, glutes, calves, feet, toes, hands. Bring that energy to every part of you, and repeat these words: _Thank you, Lord, for your blessing on this new day. May your grace guide my path, and may your essence lead me. May my intuition be an extension of your will, and may I be a vessel through which your greatness manifests._

With that said, you give thanks for the practice, stand up, and begin your day.
