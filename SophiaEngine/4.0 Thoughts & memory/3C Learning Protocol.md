# 3C Learning Protocol 
---
## Intro

This info comes from a YouTube video that i saw recently, that talks about a way to optimize you learning. Here's the link.

https://youtu.be/npQ2IORdlvU?si=_qr_iweZg9rHDPxW


The 3C Protocol to learn. According to the creator of the video, this protocol will help to allows us to improve the speed which we learn. Not only that, it actually helps with the quality of the info retention of the info we're studying. 
In simple terms, this learning protocol will allow us to learn faster and retain deeper. It's a good trade, isn't?


Let's begin with this phrase. "Your brain doesn't hate struggle, it's hunger for it"
It's a pretty deep phrase, and reveals something crucial about how the brain works. Very often, when we're studying new concepts or ideas, we make the wrong assumption of struggle with failure. It's not, actually struggle it's a good sign. It means that you are creating new neuronal pathways. It's like creating a new highway. To created, yo need government  support and budget. Then you need a bunch of workers to build the highway, and only after several days of hard work, you have your new highway. It's the same with your brain. You need the government (Your prefrontal cortex activation), the bunch of worker (your intent to try to learn the new concept) and after days or reading and practice, you create the new highway on your brain.

## 1. Compress

- Select: Select the info that is crucial and really important. You don't have to learn each concept deeply. You only have to choose the crucial information, and from that create road maps, that drives you to the next natural concepts. 
- Associate: When you're learning something new, a powerful practice is connect that new idea to something that you already now. For example, in my case, I'm learning how to program, and I'm constantly making associations between programing and [[PHILOSOPHY]], because i already have a big hard drive of philosophy info. 
- Chunk: Now comes the chunk. This is simple, take this ideas and connections and compress it into a simple model. In my case works very well short phrases, poems, metaphors or synthesis texts.

---



## 2. Compile

- Timer: This is how you manage you learning cadence. I personally use the ultradian cycle. 90 minutes of deep work and 20 minutes of break.
- Test: User regular test to proof your knowledge. You can make small daily tests, or one weekly test. That's up to you, but don't make the mistake of learn, learn and learn for several weeks and then present a giant test. That's dumb and it doesn't correlates with the way learning really happens. Ask yourself, how many times in the real word yo have te memorize enormous chunks of info? never right? so why in the hell would you do that? keep it simple.
- Tools: There's a lot of tools to learn. But i think the most powerful one, is the ones that connects with [[Feynman Principle]]. In simple terms, teach to learn.

---



## 3. Consolidate

- Micro Breaks: Inside your 90 minute block, take some 10 to 20 seconds break. That break will allow your brain to reinterpret what you're studying. Research proof that in those pauses, your brain replays what you just learned in 10 or 20 times the speed.
- Macro Breaks: The 20 minutes rest after your 90 minute deep learning block. Use this time for an active, but relaxing rest. You can meditate, do yoga, take a walk in the nature, drink a coffee or eat. Just chill a little bit and let your brain process all the info you just absorbed. 
- Rest/Sleep: Have, religiously 8 hours of sleep every night. It's shown that when you sleep, your brain repeats your day (and the learning of he)  backwards. So it's a very powerful loop. Learn regular and backwards. I really think that there's nothing else to explain here, it's not a secret that good sleep improves your general health and thinking.

---


## RESUME

- Be okey with struggle, it's a key part of learning process
- Select the most important info to work with it.
- Associate what are you learning with concepts you already now.
- Chunk the new info with simple models.
- Use regular tests to prove your knowledge.
- Use this workflow block: 90 minutes of deep work/20 minutes of macro break. Use the break to chill down and relax. You can do [[YOGA]], [[MEDITATION]], or a simple walk in the nature. The main goal is be calm and serene.
- Try to keep 8 hours of quality sleep everyday. This is great for brain health and retention.
